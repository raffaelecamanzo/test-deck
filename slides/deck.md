# Population Status and Trends

---

<!-- _class: content -->

## Uneven Recovery Patterns

<div class="columns two-col duo">
  <div>
    <h3>Rebounded Species</h3>
    <ul>
      <li>Protection enabled recovery</li>
      <li>Healthy prey and habitat access</li>
      <li>Examples: select humpback populations</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div>
    <h3>Stagnant or Declining</h3>
    <ul>
      <li>Low numbers and slow growth</li>
      <li>Chronic human pressures</li>
      <li>Examples: critically endangered species</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

---

<!-- _class: content no-top-accent -->

## Humpback Rebound vs. Critically Endangered

<div class="columns compare split-panels">
  <div class="panel">
    <h3>Humpback</h3>
    <ul>
      <li>Strong growth signals</li>
      <li>Resilience in multiple regions</li>
      <li>Evidence of recovery momentum</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="panel">
    <h3>Critically Endangered</h3>
    <ul>
      <li>Very low population counts</li>
      <li>Slow reproduction rates</li>
      <li>High sensitivity to disruption</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

---

<!-- _class: content hero hero-ranked -->

## Long Lives, Slow Recoveries

<ol class="ranked-list">
  <li>Long lifespans delay population turnover</li>
  <li>Slow reproduction limits rebound speed</li>
  <li>Late maturity stretches recovery timelines</li>
  <li>Monitoring must span decades</li>
</ol>
